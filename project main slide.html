PROTEIN FOODS

Protein is an essential macronutrient, but not all food sources of protein are created equal, and you may not need as much as you think. Learn the basics about protein and shaping your diet with healthy protein foods.

Proteins are large and complex molecules made up of amino acids.
They play a crucial role in various biological functions, including building and repairing tissues, supporting immune function, and acting as enzymes that facilitate chemical reactions in the body.


Why is protein important?

Proteins perform a wide variety of roles in the body including serving as enzymes that catalyze chemical reactions, acting as structural components of cells and tissues participating in signaling pathways, and aiding immune responses.   • Helps in overall functioning
Protein helps to build and repair muscles. It is also an energy resource, and is responsible for your growth and development. According to a study, published in The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, Metabolic reactions also take place because of protein, plus it helps in the smooth functioning of bodily functions.
  • Helps in Weight loss
Protein also helps in maintenance of weight. According to a study, published in the Journal of the American College of Nutrition, protein helps you stay full for a longer time as it produces a hormone called grehlin.

  • Good for your bones

    • Protein helps the calcium reach your body. People who have a higher intake of protein, have better bone mass and experience lesser risk of osteoporosis as well as fractures as they age, states a study, published in Journal of the American College of Nutrition.

  • Helps with heart health

    • Protein is very good for maintaining blood pressure, which helps prevent heart attacks as well as strokes. In a study, published in Plos One, systolic blood pressure and diastolic blood pressure was lowered after regular intake of protein.
